BROADEN HORIZONS - PLAYER GUIDE
===============================

Quick controls
- H: open help menu
- L: open the planet list
- Arrow keys / WASD: pan the galaxy map
- Escape: close dialogs or return to the previous screen
- Use the mouse wheel or Up/Down keys to scroll this page

Starting state
- Your starting planet begins explored with a City in the central region.
- Your first Explorer unit is ready to reveal nearby regions.
- Starting resources: Food 6, Materials 2, Science 0, Energy 20

Core resources
- Food: supports units and recruitment.
- Materials: builds and upgrades units, improvements, ships, and colonies.
- Science: unlocks technologies in the Tech Tree.
- Energy: powers advanced structures and ship missions.

Galaxy and planets
- The galaxy contains 20 planets.
- Planet size ranges from 12 to 36 regions.
- The starting planet has at least 22 regions.
- Planets are placed with a minimum distance of 150 from each other.
- Click a planet on the map to open its surface screen.

Habitat types and base output
- City:      +3 Food, +3 Materials, +1 Science, +1 Energy
- Forest:    +2 Food, +3 Materials, +1 Science
- Ocean:     +3 Food, +0 Materials, +2 Science
- Mountains: +1 Food, +3 Materials, +1 Science
- Prairie:   +3 Food, +1 Materials, +1 Science
- Valley:    +3 Food, +2 Materials, +1 Science
- Desert:    +1 Food, +2 Materials, +2 Science
- Ruins:     +1 Food, +1 Materials, +3 Science
- Volcano:   +1 Food, +3 Materials, +2 Science
- Arctic:    +1 Food, +1 Materials, +2 Science

Exploration
- Unexplored regions are hidden until an Explorer reveals them.
- Explorers can move into unexplored tiles and will take a turn to reveal them.
- Only explored regions contribute to production and resource bonuses.

Unit roles
- Explorer: reveal adjacent unexplored regions.
- Farmer: improve food generation on productive habitats.
- Miner: boost material output from rich regions.
- Scientist: increase science output and unlock research.
- Builder: construct improvements on eligible habitats.
- Harvester: increase material yield from productive habitats.
- Fishermen: boost food production in Ocean regions.
- Colonists: support colonization actions and advanced planet expansion.

Improvements
- Builders can construct improvements only on matching habitats.
- Improvements cost Materials and take multiple turns to complete.
- Example improvements:
  * Urban Development (City): +1 Food, +1 Materials, +1 Science, +1 Energy
  * Aquaculture Farm (Ocean): +2 Food
  * Science Research Stations (Desert): +2 Science, -1 Energy
  * Geothermal Power Plant (Volcano): +5 Energy
- Choose improvements that match your planet's strengths.

Region bonuses
- Each planet can have 1 to 6 region bonuses depending on size.
- Bonuses only appear on compatible habitats.
- Example region bonuses:
  * Mineral Vein: +1 Materials
  * Fertile Soil: +1 Food
  * Crystal Deposits: +1 Science
  * Gold Vein: +3 Materials
  * Quantum Crystals: +3 Science
  * Geothermal Vents: +2 Energy

Research and advancement
- Science is used to research technology upgrades.
- Technologies unlock stronger units, structures, and ship options.
- Invest in science early to improve long-term growth.

Ships and colonization
- Some ships can explore distant planets.
- Colony ships let you claim new worlds, but require Materials and Food to launch.
- Keep Energy available for ship missions and advanced operations.

Strategy tips
- Balance Food, Materials, Science, and Energy.
- Use Explorers to reveal high-value habitats early.
- Match Builders and improvements to habitat types.
- Keep enough Energy for new ships and construction.

Remember
- Click planets to manage their regions and units.
- The help window is scrollable.
- Press H or Escape to close this guide and return to the game.
